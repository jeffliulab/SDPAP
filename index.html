Automated Production

Automation Technology: Utilizing robots, artificial intelligence, and other advanced technologies to minimize human labor in the production process. Automation can significantly increase production efficiency, reduce costs, and improve product quality.

Productivity Enhancement: Fully automated production can dramatically boost productivity, enabling society to produce a wide range of goods and services more efficiently.

Social Democracy

Economic Equality: Social democracy emphasizes achieving economic equality through government intervention and social policies, ensuring everyone has access to basic life necessities and welfare.

Public Services: Social democratic states typically provide extensive public services, including healthcare, education, housing, and social welfare, to enhance citizens' quality of life.

Democratic Participation: Social democracy values citizens' democratic participation, advocating for the formulation and implementation of public policies through democratic processes.

Integration of Automated Production and Social Democracy

Wealth Distribution: In a society with fully automated production, overall wealth increases due to enhanced productivity. Social democratic policies can ensure this wealth is distributed fairly, reducing the gap between rich and poor.

Work and Income: As automation becomes widespread, traditional jobs decrease, necessitating a redefinition of work and income sources. Social democracy can provide basic income or other forms of social security to ensure everyone has the essentials for living.

Work-Life Balance: Automated production can reduce working hours, allowing people more free time for leisure, learning, and personal development, thereby improving the quality of life.

Progressivism

Progressivism is a social and political movement aimed at achieving societal improvement and advancement through social reforms and innovations. The core principles of progressivism include:

  • Social Justice: Fairness and justice, striving to eliminate inequalities in society, including those related to race, gender, economics, and education.
  • Economic Reform: Promoting economic policy reforms to ensure fair distribution of wealth and resources, reducing the gap between the rich and the poor, and supporting workers' rights and better working conditions.
  • Environmental Protection: Addressing environmental issues, advocating for sustainable development, and pushing for policies to combat climate change and protect natural resources.
  • Technological Innovation: Encouraging technological progress and innovation, supporting scientific research and education.
  • Democratic Participation: Advocating for broad citizen participation and democratic decision-making.
  • Social Welfare: Supporting a robust social security system to improve the quality of life for all members of society.

Automated Production Era

Robots and AI will replace cheap labor, and the advantages of late development and demographic dividends will no longer exist; backward countries will completely lose the possibility of progress.

The relationships between production, labor, and market will be completely overturned.

If private ownership still exists, who will the results of automated production factories belong to? Wealth inequality will further widen. Those who possess automated production capabilities will become monopolists. If SDPAP fails to seize the ideological high ground, society will slide into a cyberpunk era. In this future, companies with large-scale production capabilities will essentially colonize other regions lacking automated production capabilities.

Automated production is inevitable, and humanity's pursuit of high productivity is irreversible. If SDPAP cannot be achieved, the future trajectory will be:

  • Robots will have unparalleled advantages: precise production, tirelessness. As time goes on, robots will replace more and more jobs, leading to a continuous increase in unemployment.
  • Once the initial costs are recovered, robots will work tirelessly like thoughtless slaves, leading factories into a state of continuous overproduction.
  • When overproduction requires dumping goods, regions without automated production capabilities will be forced to open their markets, and the local populace will fall into poverty.
  • Once unemployed workers and most people in backward regions lose the possibility of labor, their labor value will become zero, and they will ultimately become "toys" and "servants."
  • The trade of human bodies and slavery will return to human society, with slaves engaging in servitude rather than labor since labor will be performed by robots. Most people's labor value will be reduced to emotional value and degradation, becoming physical toys and servants providing emotional value.
  • As biotechnology further develops, the wealthy will have more say in choosing the genes of their offspring, making their descendants both highly intelligent and physically fit, further widening the gap between the rich and the poor, elevating it from a class gap to a species gap.

However, if SDPAP can be developed in the early stages of automated production, the future will be:

  • Robots perform basic agricultural and industrial labor, producing a large amount of agricultural and industrial products that can be distributed to all people; or through universal salary, keep the economy circulating, avoiding overproduction issues.
  • The living standards of the poor rise to today's middle class, and everyone has basic living and housing security.
  • The leisure class grows unprecedentedly, accelerating the progress of the entertainment industry and scientific development.
  • As science and technology further develop, the scale of fully automated production deepens, and people's living standards rise accordingly. Society will gradually approach a flat state where everyone is affluent, and people no longer see wealth as a source of superiority but rather wisdom, scientific research, etc. New social evaluation systems will gradually be born, and scientific communes, scientific societies, and even scientific nations will emerge, with most people engaged in research rather than labor.
